# fix711 — guide genre stripe: top edge (fix708 visibility follow-up) (v4.1.15)

**Builder:** desktop. **Live base:** v4.1.14. **Ships as:** v4.1.15+711. TV only;
phone UI byte-identical. Overnight autonomous run (owner approved).

## Why
fix708 put the on-now cell's genre colour on the **left edge** (3px vertical
stripe). On-device (guide window starts at ~"now") the on-now cell begins right
at the now-line, so the left stripe sat in the thin sliver under the vertical
now-line + its glow → effectively invisible (confirmed on the onn FOX cells).

## What
`tv_guide_view.dart` `_block` — the on-now genre `Positioned` (gated `isNow`,
inside the fix705 `Clip.antiAlias` cell, painted under the title):
- FROM `left:0, top:0, bottom:0, width:3` (vertical left stripe)
- TO   `left:0, right:0, top:0, height:3` (full-width top stripe)

The now-line is vertical, so it never covers a top-edge stripe → the genre colour
reads clearly. Colour source unchanged (`genreEdgeColor(p.category)`); still
on-now-only; still clipped to the cell's top rounded corners.

## Tests
`test/fix708_genre_test.dart` updated: the wiring test now asserts the top-edge
geometry (`right:0` + `top:0` + `height:3`) and that the old left-edge vertical
form is gone. Normalizer tests unchanged. Analyze clean.
