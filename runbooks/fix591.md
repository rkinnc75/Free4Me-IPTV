# fix591 — Multi-view crash: quick re-open drifted cells onto hardware decode

**Version:** v2.2.6+593
**Date:** 2026-06-28
**Type:** Crash fix (diagnosed from the on-device diagnostic log)

## Root cause (from the 2026-06-28 onn diagnostic report)

In a 2×2 multi-view, all 4 cells correctly start in SOFTWARE decode on the
low-RAM onn — the log shows `Player: hwdec=no (preview, multiViewDecode=auto)`
for each cell (mpv_engine's `auto` already picks software on Tegra OR low-RAM).
BUT the fix341 EOF "quick re-open (same engine)" called `eng.open()` WITHOUT
re-applying mpv options, so hwdec was not re-asserted and mpv reverted to its
hardware default (`auto-safe` → `mediacodec-copy`). On a connection-cycling
provider the cells crept back onto hardware MediaCodec; 4 concurrent MediaCodec
sessions on the 2 GB onn then hit `h264_mediacodec: Both surface and
native_window are NULL` and the app took a **native crash** (cold restarts at
12:53:49 and 12:59:59 in the log, no log-clear between).

## Fix (`lib/multi_view_cell.dart`)

- Cache `_lastIgnoreSsl` at the initial `reapplyOptions`.
- In the quick re-open, call `eng.reapplyOptions(url, ignoreSsl: _lastIgnoreSsl)`
  BEFORE `eng.open()` so hwdec (and the other tuned options) are re-pinned —
  the cell stays on software decode (`hwdec=no`) across EOF recoveries and never
  drifts onto hardware MediaCodec. Mirrors the initial-open ordering.

## Verification

- `flutter analyze` clean; `flutter test` green (272).
- **On-device (onn):** open a 2×2 with connection-cycling channels (e.g. the SP
  sports cells that EOF), let it run several minutes through multiple re-opens.
  Confirm: STATS keep `hwdec-req="no"` / software (NOT `mediacodec-copy`), no
  `Both surface and native_window are NULL`, and no app restart. dumpsys can
  confirm a single stable process across the session.
