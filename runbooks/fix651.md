# fix651 — TV: hold-to-seek acceleration + coalesced engine seeks (v2.2.51)

**Builder:** desktop, applying the phone coder's work order `fix650.md`
(renumbered per VERIFY_643-647 protocol). **Live base:** v2.2.50.
**Ships as:** v2.2.51+640.

## Problem (from the work order)

Holding ◀/▶ repeat-seeks at a flat 10 s per KeyRepeat and every repeat awaits
a full mpv seek — crossing a 3-hour movie takes ~1000 repeats, and on the onn
(2 GB Amlogic) the un-batched seek stream stutters the engine.

## Change (applied verbatim, markers renumbered fix650→fix651)

- New `lib/player/seek_acceleration.dart`: pure `seekStepSeconds(repeatCount,
  duration)` ladder — 10 s (repeats 0–4), 30 s (5–9, content ≥5 min), 60 s
  (10–14, ≥30 min), 120 s (15+, ≥90 min). Unknown duration (live DVR) caps at
  30 s.
- `player.dart`: KeyDown resets the repeat count; deltas route through a
  `_requestSeek` coalescer — first request in an idle window applies
  immediately, held-key deltas accumulate and flush as ONE `engine.seek` per
  250 ms tick. The skip chip still updates per press. Overlay/phone-bar
  rewind/forward buttons route through the same coalescer. Flush timer
  cancelled in dispose.
- New `test/fix651_seek_acceleration_test.dart` pins the full ladder.

## Verify on device (onn)

1. Movie: HOLD ▶ — chip counts up in growing steps; release: position lands
   where the chip said, no stutter storm.
2. `logcat | grep "transport seek"`: while holding, at most ~4 lines/s (one
   per 250 ms flush) regardless of repeat rate — the coalescing proof.
3. Single taps: still exactly ±10 s, instant, one line each.
4. Live+DVR hold: steps never exceed 30 s.
