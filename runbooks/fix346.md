## Why (cold-eyes review HIGH-3)
One provider EOF surfaces as TWO signals — `errorStream("End of file")` and `completedStream(done)` — and the two listeners implemented DIFFERENT strategies: the error branch scheduled a full dispose+restart (spinner), the completed branch scheduled the fix341 quick re-open (frozen frame, no spinner). Which one ran was decided by Dart stream delivery order, and `_eofRetryScheduled` (the shared de-dup flag) was cleared BEFORE the quick re-open's `open()` even ran — so a second signal for the SAME event could interleave with the in-flight re-open. Result: nondeterministic recovery — sometimes smooth, sometimes spinner, sometimes both partially — undermining fix341's keep-the-last-frame guarantee and the provider connection budget.

## What this does
- **New `_onEof(ch, generation, {via})`** — the single owner. Both listeners reduce to one-line delegates (the `via` tag preserves signal provenance in the log line: `stream completed (via errorStream|completedStream)`).
- **Deterministic strategy:** quick re-open while `_quickReopens < 8`, else the full budgeted restart. No more strategy-by-race.
- **Commit-then-clear flag lifecycle:** `_eofRetryScheduled` is cleared only AFTER the quick re-open's `open()` has returned (was: cleared before open); the full-restart path is reset by `_disposeEngine`/`_startEngine` as before. A duplicate signal during the in-flight re-open is now correctly absorbed.
- The fix345 CRIT-1 position-advance probe is preserved unchanged inside the quick path.
- Net behaviour: identical to the GOOD path of fix341 every time; the racing full-restart variant is gone. No settings, no migration.
