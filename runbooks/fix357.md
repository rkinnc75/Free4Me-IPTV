## What this does

### Live DVR buffer (spec: Rich, 2026-06-12)
- **Settings:** `Live DVR buffer (single view)` toggle (default **OFF**) + `DVR length (minutes)` slider **5–90 in 5-minute steps, default 5**. Full plumbing (model, persistence, backup export/import, reset) + help text.
- **Scope:** full-screen live TV only — gated on `dvrEligible` (set ONLY by the full-screen Player when there is no catch-up override), `!previewMode`, and `mediaType == livestream`. Multi-view cells and the mini-player never get it.
- **Mechanism:** mpv disk-backed cache, stream-copy. `cache-on-disk=yes` into `<tmp>/free4me_dvr`, `demuxer-max-back-bytes` sized to the window, `force-seekable=yes` so the recorded window is seekable — pausing live TV builds a cushion that brief drops play through. **Codec decision:** the stream is stored bit-identical (no re-encode); real-time transcoding is not feasible on these devices, so stream-copy IS the most space-efficient viable option (~30–60 MB/min depending on channel; sizing estimate 60 MB/min ≈ 8 Mbps).
- **Disk safety (the "stop 5 minutes short" rule):** at open, free space is read via toybox `df -k`; the window is capped to `(free ÷ 60 MB/min) − 5 minutes`; under 5 minutes of headroom the DVR disables itself with a WARN. A 60-second guard then measures the cache's REAL growth rate and free disk; if free space falls under 5 minutes' worth at the measured rate, the back buffer is frozen at its current size (growth stops, playback continues). Cache directory is wiped on engine teardown.
- The pre-existing live `force-seekable=no` seek-probe suppression remains the non-DVR default — byte-identical behaviour when the toggle is off.

### Version stamp on log clear/export
`AppLog.stampVersion(context)` writes `Free4Me-IPTV <version>+<build> — <context>`; called after every log clear (manual or version-change wipe) and at the start of every TV log export — a log file can no longer exist without identifying its version (the 2026-06-12 Shield log had none).
